Mons Olympian

Project for User Interface Design by Tanaka Chingonzo, Jack De la Cruz, and Ivan Jaramillo

What This Project Is

This is a Unity-based game that tries to be the space version of The Oregon Trail.

Our target audience is softly focused on students in middle and high school. This will allow us to strengthen core physics concepts in youth while not being annoying to adults who have not studied space exploration. The United States has been rejecting science and pushing researchers aside for years which is detrimental to our society as a whole. To ensure the United States is not ignorant on the basics of science, we must begin educating the youth while reinforcing the education through examples of applications.

Why This Project Matters

Space travel has notoriously been too expensive and too dangerous to send humans on a months long, interplanetary mission to mars. However, with a renewed and energetic interest in private companies like SpaceX and Blue Origin a trip to Mars for by the average person is becoming a realistic possibility in our lifetime. This implies a need for enhanced educational tools in educating the next generation of astronauts, scientists, and engineers.
